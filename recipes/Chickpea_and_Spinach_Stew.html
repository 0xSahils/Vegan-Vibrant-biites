Chickpea and Spinach Stew

This Chickpea and Spinach Stew is a hearty, nutritious, and flavorful vegan dish, perfect for those chilly nights when you crave something warm and comforting. Packed with protein-rich chickpeas and fiber-loaded spinach, this stew offers a satisfying meal that’s both nourishing and delicious. The rich blend of spices, including cumin, coriander, turmeric, and paprika, adds depth to the stew, while garlic and onions provide a savory base. The tomatoes lend a touch of tanginess, balancing the earthiness of the chickpeas and the freshness of the spinach. Not only is this stew comforting, but it’s also packed with essential vitamins and minerals, making it a great option for boosting your immune system. The dish is naturally gluten-free and can easily be adapted for a variety of dietary preferences. Serve it over brown rice or with a slice of crusty bread for a complete meal. Whether you’re enjoying it on a cozy evening or packing it for meal prep, this stew is a winner. It’s a perfect example of how simple, plant-based ingredients can come together to create a hearty, flavorful, and wholesome dish that everyone will love.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups chickpeas: Either cooked from dried beans or canned (drained and rinsed) for convenience.
  • 4 cups fresh spinach: Washed thoroughly and roughly chopped for easy cooking and a boost of nutrients.
  • 1 medium onion: Diced, providing a sweet and savory base flavor.
  • 3 garlic cloves: Minced to infuse the stew with a bold and aromatic taste.
  • 2 cups vegetable broth: (How to make Vegetable Broth here).
  • 1 can diced tomatoes: With juice, contributing a tangy and slightly sweet flavor to balance the stew's earthiness.
  • 1 tbsp olive oil: For sautéing, adding healthy fats and helping to bring out the flavors of the spices.
  • 1 tsp cumin: Ground cumin to add a warm, earthy flavor that complements the chickpeas.
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika: Provides a subtle smokiness, enhancing the stew's depth and complexity.
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric: Optional, but adds a golden color and a mild, earthy flavor, along with its anti-inflammatory benefits.
  • Salt and pepper: To taste, bringing balance and enhancing the natural flavors of the ingredients.

Instructions

  1. Sauté aromatics: Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion and minced garlic, cooking until soft and fragrant, about 3-4 minutes.
  2. Add spices: Stir in the ground cumin, smoked paprika, and optional turmeric. Cook for 1 minute to allow the spices to bloom and release their flavors.
  3. Add liquids and chickpeas: Pour in the vegetable broth and diced tomatoes (with juice), then add the chickpeas. Stir and bring the mixture to a simmer. Let it cook for 15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  4. Incorporate spinach: Add the chopped spinach to the pot, stirring until it wilts and is evenly incorporated into the stew. This will take about 2-3 minutes.
  5. Season and serve: Taste the stew and adjust the salt and pepper as needed. Serve hot with a side of crusty bread, rice, or a light salad for a complete meal.

Nutrition Information (per serving)

  • Calories: 250
  • Protein: 10g
  • Carbohydrates: 30g
  • Fat: 8g
  • Fiber: 8g
