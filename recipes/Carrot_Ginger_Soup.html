Vegan Carrot Ginger Soup

This Vegan Carrot Ginger Soup is a nourishing and vibrant dish that brings together the earthy sweetness of carrots and the invigorating warmth of fresh ginger. Silky smooth and richly colored, this soup is a feast for both the eyes and the palate. Perfect for cozying up on chilly days, its creamy texture is achieved without the use of dairy, making it an ideal choice for those following a plant-based diet. The addition of aromatic spices like cumin or a pinch of cinnamon enhances the depth of flavor, while a squeeze of fresh orange juice or lemon adds a refreshing tang. Served with a crusty slice of whole-grain bread or a sprinkle of fresh herbs like cilantro or parsley, this soup is as versatile as it is delicious. Whether you’re looking for a comforting appetizer or a light main course, this Vegan Carrot Ginger Soup is a wholesome, satisfying option that’s sure to please. Plus, it’s packed with vitamins, antioxidants, and immune-boosting properties, making it a nutritious choice for any time of year.

Ingredients

  • 6 large carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp ground turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/4 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, firm pressed tofu (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Sauté the vegetables: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, garlic, and grated ginger. Sauté for 5-7 minutes until the onions become translucent and the ginger becomes fragrant.
  2. Cook the carrots: Add the chopped carrots, turmeric, cumin, and cinnamon to the pot. Stir to combine, then pour in the vegetable broth. Bring the soup to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for 25-30 minutes, or until the carrots are tender.
  3. Blend the soup: Once the carrots are cooked, use an immersion blender to blend the soup until smooth. Alternatively, transfer the soup in batches to a blender and blend until creamy.
  4. Finish the soup: Stir in the coconut milk, and season with salt and pepper to taste. Let the soup simmer for an additional 5 minutes to heat through.
  5. Tofu:Cut the tofu in small cubed size and Roast the tofu in the pan with little dissle of olive oil
  6. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls, garnish with fresh cilantro and fried tofu if desired and with some rosted tofu, and serve hot.

Nutrition Information (per serving)

  • Calories: 180
  • Protein
  • Carbohydrates: 30g
  • Fat: 7g
  • Fiber: 5g
