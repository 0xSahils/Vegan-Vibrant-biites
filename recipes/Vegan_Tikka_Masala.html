Vegan Tikka Masala

Experience a symphony of spices and rich flavors with our Vegan Tikka Masala. Creamy, vibrant, and satisfying, this dish combines perfectly marinated tofu with a velvety tomato-coconut sauce. Ideal for cozy dinners, this recipe is best enjoyed with fragrant basmati rice or soft, warm naan.

Ingredients

  • For the tofu marinade:
    • 1 block (14 oz) extra-firm tofu, pressed and cut into bite-sized cubes
    • 3/4 cup unsweetened coconut yogurt for creaminess
    • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
    • 1 tablespoon garlic, finely grated
    • 1 tablespoon ginger, finely grated
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin for earthy flavor
    • 1 teaspoon ground coriander for aromatic depth
    • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder for vibrant color
    • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika for a smoky undertone
    • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder (adjust based on your spice preference)
    • 1/2 teaspoon salt to enhance flavor
  • For the tikka masala sauce:
    • 2 tablespoons coconut oil or neutral cooking oil
    • 1 large yellow onion, finely chopped
    • 3 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
    • 1 teaspoon garam masala for warm spice notes
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper for a touch of heat (optional)
    • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
    • 1 cup full-fat coconut milk for a rich and creamy base
    • 1 teaspoon maple syrup or sugar to balance the acidity
    • Salt and pepper to taste
  • To serve:
    • Steamed basmati rice or naan bread
    • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Press and cube the tofu:

    Begin by pressing the tofu to remove excess water. This helps it absorb the flavorful marinade better. Once pressed, cut into bite-sized cubes.

  2. Marinate the tofu:

    In a large mixing bowl, combine coconut yogurt, lemon juice, garlic, ginger, cumin, coriander, turmeric, paprika, chili powder, and salt. Stir until smooth. Add the tofu cubes, ensuring each piece is generously coated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for 30 minutes to 2 hours.

  3. Cook the tofu:

    Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat with a drizzle of oil. Arrange the marinated tofu in a single layer and cook for 3-4 minutes on each side until golden and slightly crisp. Remove from the pan and set aside.

  4. Prepare the masala base:

    In the same skillet, heat coconut oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and saut√© until golden and soft, about 5-7 minutes. Stir in garlic and ginger, cooking for an additional 2 minutes.

  5. Add spices to the base:

    Sprinkle garam masala, cumin, paprika, turmeric, and cayenne pepper into the skillet. Stir well, allowing the spices to toast for 1 minute, releasing their aromatic oils.

  6. Simmer the sauce:

    Pour in the crushed tomatoes, coconut milk, and maple syrup (or sugar). Stir until smooth. Let the sauce simmer on low heat for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens and becomes creamy.

  7. Add tofu to the sauce:

    Gently fold the cooked tofu into the simmering sauce, ensuring it's evenly coated. Allow it to cook for another 5 minutes to absorb the flavors.

  8. Finish and serve:

    Sprinkle freshly chopped cilantro over the top for a burst of freshness. Serve hot with basmati rice or naan, and enjoy this flavorful delight.
