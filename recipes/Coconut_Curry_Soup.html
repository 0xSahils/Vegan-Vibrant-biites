Coconut Curry Soup

This Coconut Curry Soup is a delightful fusion of creamy coconut milk and bold, aromatic curry spices that create a perfect balance of richness and warmth. Packed with vibrant vegetables, fragrant herbs, and just the right amount of spice, this soup is both comforting and satisfying. Its silky texture and deep flavors make it an ideal choice for cozy nights or when you’re craving something hearty yet light. Perfect for meal prep or a quick weeknight dinner, it’s a versatile dish that pairs wonderfully with rice, noodles, or crusty bread. Loaded with nutrients, this vegan soup is as nourishing as it is delicious, offering a burst of exotic flavors in every spoonful. Whether you’re serving it as a starter or enjoying it as a main course, this Coconut Curry Soup is sure to become a household favorite for its ease, flavor, and wholesome goodness.

Ingredients

  • 1 can coconut milk: Full-fat for creaminess.
  • 1 onion: Chopped.
  • 2 cloves garlic: Minced.
  • 1 tbsp curry powder: Adjust to taste.
  • 2 cups vegetable broth: For the soup base. (How to make vegan broth here).
  • 1 cup carrots: Sliced.
  • 1 cup cauliflower: Chopped.
  • Salt and pepper: To taste.
  • Roasted Tofu: How to make roasted Tofu
  • Fresh cilantro: For garnish.

Instructions

  1. Sauté aromatics: Heat a large pot over medium heat and add a splash of oil. Sauté the diced onion and minced garlic until fragrant and translucent, about 3-4 minutes.
  2. Add spices: Stir in the curry powder and cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly to release its aroma and deepen the flavor.
  3. Add liquids and vegetables: Pour in the coconut milk and vegetable broth, then add the chopped carrots and cauliflower florets. Stir well to combine.
  4. Simmer the soup: Bring the mixture to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and let it simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and infused with the curry flavors.
  5. Adjust seasoning: Taste the soup and season with salt and pepper as needed. For extra heat, add a pinch of red pepper flakes or a dash of hot sauce.
  6. Serve and garnish: Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh cilantro, lime wedges, or a drizzle of coconut cream for an added touch And add rosted tofu at the top. Serve hot with crusty bread, steamed rice, or noodles for a complete meal.

Nutrition Information (per serving)

  • Calories: 220
  • Protein: 3g
  • Carbohydrates: 25g
  • Fat: 14g
  • Fiber: 4g
