Vegan Meatballs

These Vegan Meatballs are the perfect combination of savory flavors and hearty textures, made from plant-based ingredients like lentils, breadcrumbs, and spices. They’re crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, making them a perfect addition to your favorite pasta, sub sandwich, or served on their own with a dipping sauce. Packed with protein and fiber, these meatballs are a satisfying, guilt-free option that everyone, whether vegan or not, will love. The rich flavor comes from a blend of ingredients like garlic, onion, and Italian herbs, providing that familiar comforting taste. The use of lentils makes them not only delicious but also nutrient-dense, while the breadcrumbs help achieve that ideal texture. Whether you’re enjoying them with marinara sauce and spaghetti or as a snack with some spicy mustard, these vegan meatballs will quickly become a household favorite. Perfect for meal prep, these meatballs can be made in advance and stored in the fridge or freezer, making them an easy and convenient meal option for busy days. You can also get creative with the recipe, adding your favorite seasonings or swapping lentils for other beans. Whatever way you choose to serve them, these vegan meatballs are sure to bring a satisfying, plant-based twist to any meal!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cooked lentils
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs
  • 2 tbsp flaxseed meal (mixed with 5 tbsp water)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Prepare the flax egg: Mix flaxseed meal with water and let it sit for 5 minutes to thicken.
  3. Blend the mixture: In a food processor, combine lentils, oats, breadcrumbs, garlic, onion, soy sauce, smoked paprika, oregano, salt, and pepper. Blend until the mixture holds together but is not completely smooth.
  4. Shape the meatballs: Use your hands to shape the mixture into small balls. Place them on a parchment-lined baking sheet.
  5. Bake: Drizzle the meatballs with olive oil and bake for 20-25 minutes, turning them halfway through, until golden brown.
  6. Serve: Serve the meatballs with marinara sauce, pasta, or as an appetizer. Enjoy!

Nutrition Information (per serving)

  • Calories: 220
  • Protein: 8g
  • Carbohydrates: 25g
  • Fat: 7g
  • Fiber: 6g
  • Sodium: 320mg
