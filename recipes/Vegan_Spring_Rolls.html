Vegan Spring Rolls

These Vegan Spring Rolls are a fresh, crunchy, and healthy appetizer or snack, perfect for any occasion. Packed with colorful vegetables like bell peppers, carrots, cucumber, and fresh herbs, they are wrapped in rice paper and served with a creamy and savory peanut dipping sauce. They are light yet satisfying and perfect for meal prep. Vegan, gluten-free, and packed with flavor, these spring rolls are ideal for anyone looking for a quick and nutritious bite.

Ingredients

  • 10 rice paper wrappers
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1/2 cucumber, julienned
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 yellow bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro leaves
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves
  • 1/2 avocado, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup cooked rice noodles (optional)
  • 1/4 cup toasted sesame seeds (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for brushing)

    • Peanut Dipping Sauce

  • 1/4 cup peanut butter (creamy)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 2-3 tablespoons warm water (to thin the sauce)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the vegetables: Shred the carrots, julienne the cucumber, and thinly slice the bell peppers. Set the vegetables aside. If using rice noodles, cook them according to the package instructions and set aside to cool.
  2. Prepare the rice paper wrappers: Fill a large shallow dish with warm water. Dip each rice paper wrapper in the water for about 5-10 seconds until it becomes soft and pliable. Be careful not to over-soak the wrapper, as it may tear.
  3. Assemble the spring rolls: Place a wet rice paper wrapper on a clean, flat surface (like a cutting board). Lay a small handful of each vegetable, fresh herbs, avocado slices, and rice noodles (if using) along the center of the wrapper. Add a pinch of sesame seeds if desired.
  4. Roll the spring rolls: Carefully fold the sides of the rice paper wrapper over the filling. Then, tightly roll the wrapper from the bottom, like a burrito, until it forms a neat roll. Repeat with the remaining wrappers and fillings.
  5. Brush with olive oil: Lightly brush the finished spring rolls with olive oil to give them a nice sheen. This step is optional but helps with presentation.
  6. Serve: Serve the spring rolls with a peanut dipping sauce (recipe below). Enjoy immediately, or store them in the fridge for up to 2-3 hours before serving.

Instructions for Peanut Dipping Sauce:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together the peanut butter, soy sauce, maple syrup, rice vinegar, lime juice, and sesame oil until smooth.
  2. If the sauce is too thick, add warm water a little at a time until you reach the desired consistency.
  3. Serve the peanut dipping sauce alongside the spring rolls.

Nutrition Information (per serving, 1 spring roll with sauce)

  • Calories: 120
  • Protein: 3g
  • Carbohydrates: 18g
  • Fat: 7g
  • Fiber: 3g
  • Sodium: 250mg
  • Vitamin A: 45% DV
  • Iron: 6% DV
