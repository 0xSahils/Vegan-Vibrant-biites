Stuffed Bell Peppers

These Stuffed Bell Peppers are a vibrant and wholesome vegan meal that’s packed with flavor and nutrition. The colorful bell peppers are perfectly roasted to tender perfection, creating the ideal vessel for a hearty filling. Inside, you’ll find a delicious mix of quinoa, black beans, and sautéed vegetables like onions, corn, and tomatoes, all seasoned with a variety of spices that elevate the dish to new heights. The quinoa provides a satisfying base, while the black beans offer a boost of protein and fiber, making this dish both filling and nourishing. The spices—such as cumin, paprika, and garlic—create a savory, slightly smoky flavor that pairs wonderfully with the natural sweetness of the peppers. This recipe is perfect for a family dinner, as it can be easily customized with your favorite vegetables or toppings, such as avocado or vegan cheese. It's also a fantastic option for meal prepping, as the stuffed peppers keep well in the fridge for several days. Whether you're making them for a weeknight meal or as a dish to impress guests, these stuffed bell peppers are sure to delight with their burst of vibrant colors, satisfying textures, and mouthwatering taste. Serve them with a side salad or some vegan sour cream for a complete and delicious meal.

Ingredients

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color, preferably red, yellow, or orange for a sweeter flavor)
  • 1 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1 cup black beans (cooked or canned, rinsed)
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup corn kernels
  • 1/4 cup diced onions
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Peppers: Start by preheating your oven to 375°F (190°C). While the oven heats up, take your bell peppers and slice off the tops, making sure to leave enough of the pepper so it can hold the filling. Next, carefully remove the seeds and the white membranes from the inside of the peppers. Set the peppers aside.
  2. Sauté the Filling: In a large skillet or pan, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the diced onions to the pan and sauté them for about 3-4 minutes until they become soft and translucent. Then, add the diced tomatoes, corn kernels, black beans, cooked quinoa, cumin, smoked paprika, and season with salt and pepper. Stir everything together and cook for about 5 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend and heat through. The filling should be warm and slightly softened.
  3. Stuff the Peppers: Once the filling is ready, take the prepared bell peppers and spoon the filling mixture into each one. Pack the mixture in gently, but don't overfill or press down too hard. Arrange the stuffed peppers upright in a baking dish. They should stand tall, ensuring they’re supported as they cook.
  4. Bake: Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil to keep the moisture in while the peppers bake. Place the dish in the preheated oven and bake for 30 minutes. After 30 minutes, remove the foil and continue baking for an additional 10 minutes. This will help to give the peppers a slightly roasted finish and enhance their flavor.
  5. Serve: Once the peppers are done baking, remove them from the oven. Garnish the stuffed peppers with freshly chopped cilantro for a burst of flavor and color. Serve them warm and enjoy!

Nutrition Information (per serving)

  • Calories: 220
  • Protein: 8g
  • Carbohydrates: 35g
  • Fat: 6g
  • Fiber: 10g
  • Vitamin C: 150% of Daily Value
