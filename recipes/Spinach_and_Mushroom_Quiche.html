Spinach and Mushroom Quiche

Delight in the savory goodness of this Vegan Spinach and Mushroom Quiche, a perfect centerpiece for breakfast, brunch, or even a light dinner. This plant-based version of the classic quiche is packed with wholesome ingredients and bursting with flavor. The rich and creamy filling is made with a blend of silken tofu or cashews, seasoned with nutritional yeast, garlic, and a pinch of nutmeg for a cheesy and umami-packed flavor. Fresh spinach and sautéed mushrooms add a hearty, earthy touch and create a beautiful contrast of textures. The filling is encased in a flaky, golden-brown crust made with plant-based butter or coconut oil, ensuring a perfect bite every time. Easy to customize, this quiche pairs well with your favorite herbs, such as thyme or chives, and can include other veggies like bell peppers or onions for added variety. Serve it warm or at room temperature, accompanied by a crisp side salad or roasted vegetables for a complete meal. Perfect for entertaining or meal prep, Vegan Spinach and Mushroom Quiche is a satisfying dish that’s as nourishing as it is delicious. It’s sure to impress vegans and non-vegans alike with its rich flavors and hearty appeal.

Ingredients

  • For the Crust:
    • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour (or gluten-free flour blend)
    • 1/4 teaspoon salt
    • 1/2 cup vegan butter or coconut oil (cold, cut into small cubes)
    • 3-4 tablespoons ice-cold water
  • For the Filling:
    • 1 block (14 ounces) silken tofu or 1 cup soaked raw cashews (blended until smooth)
    • 2 tablespoons nutritional yeast (for a cheesy flavor)
    • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon nutmeg (optional, for a warm undertone)
    • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Vegetables:
    • 2 cups fresh spinach (roughly chopped)
    • 1 cup mushrooms (sliced, such as button, cremini, or shiitake)
    • 1 small onion (diced)
    • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • Optional Add-ins:
    • 1/2 cup vegan cheese (shredded, for extra creaminess)
    • 1/4 cup fresh herbs (chopped, such as thyme, parsley, or chives)
    • 1/4 cup diced bell peppers or sun-dried tomatoes (for added flavor)
  • For Cooking:
    • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for sautéing the vegetables)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the crust:
    1. In a large bowl, mix the flour and salt together.
    2. Add the cold vegan butter or coconut oil and use a fork or pastry cutter to combine until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
    3. Gradually add ice-cold water, one tablespoon at a time, mixing until the dough comes together.
    4. Form the dough into a ball, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  2. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease a 9-inch pie dish or tart pan.
  3. Roll out the crust: On a lightly floured surface, roll the chilled dough into a circle slightly larger than your pie dish. Carefully transfer it to the dish, pressing it into the bottom and sides. Trim any excess and prick the base with a fork. Bake the crust for 10 minutes, then set it aside.
  4. Sauté the vegetables:
    1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat.
    2. Add the onions and cook until translucent, about 3-4 minutes.
    3. Stir in the mushrooms and garlic, cooking until softened and fragrant, about 5 minutes.
    4. Add the spinach and cook until wilted. Remove from heat and set aside to cool slightly.
  5. Prepare the filling:
    1. In a blender or food processor, combine silken tofu (or soaked cashews), nutritional yeast, garlic powder, nutmeg, salt, and black pepper. Blend until smooth and creamy.
    2. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  6. Assemble the quiche:
    1. In a large bowl, combine the sautéed vegetables with the tofu or cashew mixture. Stir well to combine.
    2. If using optional add-ins like vegan cheese or herbs, fold them into the mixture.
  7. Fill the crust: Pour the filling into the pre-baked crust, spreading it evenly with a spatula.
  8. Bake the quiche: Bake in the preheated oven for 35-40 minutes, or until the filling is set and slightly golden on top.
  9. Cool and serve: Let the quiche cool for at least 10 minutes before slicing. Serve warm or at room temperature. Enjoy!
