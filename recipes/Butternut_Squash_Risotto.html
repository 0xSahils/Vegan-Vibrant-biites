Butternut Squash Risotto

This creamy Butternut Squash Risotto is a delightful fusion of flavors and textures, perfect for cozy nights and special occasions alike. The natural sweetness of roasted butternut squash is beautifully complemented by the savory depth of sautéed garlic and onions. With its rich, velvety consistency, this dish is both comforting and indulgent, making it a fantastic choice for family dinners or an elegant main course for guests. The Arborio rice absorbs the flavors of vegetable broth and aromatic spices, creating a harmonious blend in every bite. Garnished with fresh herbs and a sprinkle of vegan Parmesan, this risotto is as visually appealing as it is delicious. Whether you’re looking for a warm, hearty meal to brighten a chilly evening or a dish that’s sure to impress, this Butternut Squash Risotto is a winner. Plus, it’s entirely plant-based and packed with nutrients, making it a wholesome choice for anyone seeking a healthy yet satisfying recipe.

Ingredients

  • 1 small butternut squash: Peeled, seeded, and cubed.
  • 1 tbsp olive oil: For roasting the squash and sautéing the aromatics.
  • 1 small onion: Chopped.
  • 2 cloves garlic: Minced.
  • 1 cup Arborio rice: The key ingredient for the creamy risotto texture.
  • 3 cups vegetable broth: Use warm broth for a smooth cooking process. (How to make vegan broth here).
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine: Optional, for depth of flavor.
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast: For a cheesy, savory flavor.
  • Salt and pepper: To taste.
  • Fresh thyme: For garnish.

Instructions

  1. Roast the squash: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss the butternut squash cubes in olive oil, salt, and pepper. Spread evenly on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes until tender and slightly caramelized.
  2. Prepare the risotto base: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté for 3-4 minutes until fragrant and softened.
  3. Cook the rice: Stir in the Arborio rice and cook for 1-2 minutes, until the rice is lightly toasted.
  4. Add the broth: Gradually add the vegetable broth, about 1/2 cup at a time, stirring continuously until the liquid is absorbed before adding more broth. Continue this process until the rice is creamy and tender, about 20-25 minutes.
  5. Finish the risotto: Once the rice is cooked, stir in the roasted butternut squash, nutritional yeast, and dry white wine (if using). Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve: Spoon the risotto onto plates and garnish with fresh thyme. Serve hot and enjoy!
