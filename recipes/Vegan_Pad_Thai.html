Vegan Pad Thai

Vegan Pad Thai is a vibrant and flavorful Thai dish made with stir-fried rice noodles, crunchy vegetables, tofu, and a tangy-sweet sauce. This plant-based version of the classic Pad Thai captures the essence of the original dish with a perfect balance of flavors—salty, sweet, sour, and a little bit spicy. The rice noodles are cooked until soft and slightly caramelized, while the tofu adds a crispy texture and protein. The Pad Thai sauce is made with tamarind paste, soy sauce, lime juice, and a hint of sweetness from maple syrup or coconut sugar. This sauce is key to achieving that distinctive Pad Thai taste, coating the noodles and veggies in a rich, tangy glaze. Fresh vegetables like carrots, bell peppers, and green onions add crunch and color, while roasted peanuts and cilantro bring extra flavor and texture to the dish. Vegan Pad Thai is a quick and easy dish that’s perfect for lunch or dinner. It’s highly customizable—add in extra veggies like bean sprouts or bok choy, or adjust the spiciness to your taste with chili flakes. Whether you enjoy it mild or spicy, this dish is packed with bold flavors that are sure to satisfy your cravings for authentic Thai cuisine, all while being completely plant-based and wholesome.

Ingredients

  • 8 oz rice noodles
  • 1 block extra-firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 1/2 cup chopped scallions
  • 1/4 cup chopped peanuts
  • 2 tbsp cilantro, chopped
  • For the sauce:
  • 3 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tsp chili flakes
  • 1 clove garlic, minced

Instructions

  1. Cook the noodles: Boil the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and rinse under cold water to prevent sticking.
  2. Prepare the tofu: Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the tofu cubes and cook until golden on all sides. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  3. Make the sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together tamarind paste, soy sauce, maple syrup, chili flakes, and garlic.
  4. Stir-fry the veggies: Heat the remaining oil in the skillet. Add the shredded carrots and bean sprouts, cooking for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Combine everything: Add the cooked noodles, tofu, and sauce to the skillet. Toss until everything is evenly coated and heated through.
  6. Garnish and serve: Top with chopped peanuts, cilantro, and scallions. Serve immediately.

Nutrition Information (per serving)

  • Calories: 360
  • Protein: 12g
  • Carbohydrates: 50g
  • Fat: 10g
  • Fiber: 4g
  • Sodium: 600mg
