Vegan Cabbage Stir Fry

This Vegan Cabbage Stir Fry is a quick, flavorful, and nutritious dish that comes together in just minutes. The cabbage is saut√©ed to a perfect crisp-tender texture, absorbing the savory flavors of garlic, ginger, and soy sauce. Vibrant bell peppers, carrots, and green onions add crunch and color, making it a visually appealing dish as well as a healthy one. The stir fry is naturally gluten-free and packed with fiber, vitamins, and minerals, making it a great option for a light lunch or a side dish. With a hint of sesame oil and a touch of chili flakes, this cabbage stir fry is both comforting and satisfying, offering a delightful mix of flavors that are sure to please everyone at the table.

Ingredients

  • 1 small cabbage: Shredded.
  • 1 red bell pepper: Sliced thinly.
  • 1 yellow bell pepper: Sliced thinly.
  • 1 medium onion: Thinly sliced.
  • 2 cloves garlic: Minced.
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce: For the sauce.
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar: Adds a bit of tanginess.
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil: For stir frying.
  • 1 teaspoon ginger: Grated fresh ginger for flavor.
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds: For garnish.
  • Optional: Chili flakes or Sriracha for a spicy kick.

Instructions

  1. Prepare the vegetables: Shred the cabbage, and slice the bell peppers, onion, and garlic. Grate the fresh ginger.
  2. Heat the oil: In a large pan or wok, heat the sesame oil over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the onion and garlic. Stir-fry for 2 minutes until the onion becomes translucent.
  3. Add bell peppers: Add the red and yellow bell peppers to the pan and stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until they start to soften.
  4. Add cabbage: Add the shredded cabbage to the pan and stir everything together. Cook for another 4-5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the cabbage softens but still has a bit of crunch.
  5. Make the sauce: In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, and grated ginger.
  6. Toss the stir fry: Pour the sauce over the vegetables and toss to combine. Continue to cook for 2 more minutes to allow the sauce to absorb and coat the vegetables.
  7. Serve: Remove from heat, sprinkle with sesame seeds, and serve immediately. Optionally, add chili flakes or a drizzle of Sriracha for extra spice.
