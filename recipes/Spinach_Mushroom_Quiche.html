Vegan Spinach Mushroom Quiche

This Vegan Spinach Mushroom Quiche is a delightful and hearty dish that’s perfect for brunch, lunch, or a light dinner. Made with a flaky, golden-brown crust and a creamy tofu-based filling, this quiche is a satisfying and wholesome alternative to traditional quiche, without the use of eggs or dairy. The savory filling is packed with tender sautéed mushrooms, fresh spinach, and a blend of spices that bring out the rich, umami flavor. The tofu-based filling mimics the smooth, custard-like texture of classic quiche, while providing a boost of protein and a creamy consistency. Whether you're serving it for a special occasion or simply enjoying it as a weeknight meal, this vegan quiche is a crowd-pleaser. It’s versatile too—feel free to experiment with adding other vegetables like onions, peppers, or zucchini, or even a sprinkle of dairy-free cheese for extra richness. Serve it warm or chilled for a deliciously satisfying meal that everyone will enjoy. With its vibrant flavors and satisfying texture, this Vegan Spinach Mushroom Quiche is sure to become a favorite in your recipe collection.

Ingredients

  • 1 pre-made vegan pie crust: Or make your own with flour, vegan butter, and cold water.
  • 2 cups fresh spinach: Washed and chopped.
  • 1 cup mushrooms: Sliced (button or cremini works best).
  • 1/2 cup onion: Diced.
  • 1 block firm tofu: Drained and crumbled.
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast: For cheesy flavor.
  • 2 tbsp olive oil: For sautéing the vegetables.
  • 1 tsp garlic powder: Adds savory flavor.
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric: For color and mild flavor.
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce: For depth of flavor.
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard: For tanginess.
  • 1/2 tsp salt: To taste.
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper: To taste.
  • 1/4 cup non-dairy milk: Unsweetened almond or soy milk works well.

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Sauté the vegetables: Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add onions and mushrooms, and sauté for about 5 minutes until softened. Add spinach and cook for an additional 2 minutes until wilted. Remove from heat and set aside.
  3. Prepare the tofu filling: In a large bowl, crumble the tofu. Add nutritional yeast, garlic powder, turmeric, soy sauce, Dijon mustard, salt, pepper, and non-dairy milk. Mix well until the mixture becomes smooth and creamy.
  4. Combine the filling and vegetables: Add the sautéed vegetables to the tofu mixture and stir to combine.
  5. Assemble the quiche: Pour the tofu and vegetable mixture into the prepared pie crust. Spread it evenly and press it down lightly.
  6. Bake: Place the quiche in the oven and bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the filling is set.
  7. Cool and serve: Allow the quiche to cool for 10 minutes before slicing. Serve warm or at room temperature, and enjoy!
