Miso Ramen

Dive into the warmth of this vegan miso ramen, a dish that brings together the rich umami flavors of miso broth with hearty noodles and fresh vegetables. This comforting bowl is elevated by the addition of tofu for protein and an array of vibrant toppings like scallions, sesame seeds, and nori. The miso broth is a harmonious blend of savory and slightly sweet flavors, made with miso paste, soy sauce, and a touch of ginger for depth. Fresh veggies like mushrooms, bok choy, and carrots add texture and nutrients, while the tofu cubes soak up the flavorful broth for a satisfying bite. Perfect for a chilly day or when you’re craving something soothing yet energizing, this ramen is as versatile as it is delicious. Non-vegans can opt to add a soft-boiled egg for a traditional touch, but the dish is equally delightful without it. Whether you prefer spicy chili oil, a squeeze of lime, or extra garlic as a topping, this ramen can be easily tailored to suit your palate. Enjoy this wholesome and nourishing meal that combines the best of Japanese-inspired comfort food with plant-based goodness.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups vegetable broth: A savory base for the ramen. Use store-bought or homemade for richer flavor. (Learn how to make vegan broth here).
  • 3 tbsp white miso paste: Provides the signature umami flavor and depth to the broth.
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce: Enhances the broth’s savory profile with a touch of saltiness.
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil: Adds a nutty aroma and silky texture to the dish.
  • 2 garlic cloves: Minced for a bold, aromatic kick.
  • 1-inch piece of ginger: Grated to infuse the broth with warmth and spice.
  • 200g ramen noodles: Choose your favorite brand for the perfect chewy texture.
  • 1 block firm tofu: Pressed to remove excess water and cubed for protein-packed bites.
  • 1/2 cup sliced mushrooms: Shiitake or button mushrooms work wonderfully, adding earthiness and texture.
  • 1/2 cup baby spinach: For a touch of freshness and vibrant color.
  • 2 green onions: Chopped finely for garnish and a mild onion flavor.
  • 1/2 tsp chili oil (optional): Drizzle for a spicy, flavorful kick.

Instructions

  1. Prepare the tofu: Press the tofu to remove excess water, then cut it into cubes. In a pan, heat a little oil and pan-fry the tofu until crispy on all sides. Set aside.
  2. Make the broth: In a large pot, heat the sesame oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and ginger and sauté for 2 minutes. Add the vegetable broth, miso paste, soy sauce, and stir until the miso paste dissolves. Bring to a simmer and cook for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Cook the noodles: Cook the ramen noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  4. Assemble the ramen: Divide the cooked noodles into bowls. Pour the miso broth over the noodles. Add the sautéed tofu, mushrooms, spinach, and boiled eggs (if using). Garnish with green onions, chili oil, and any other toppings you prefer.
  5. Serve: Serve hot and enjoy your delicious vegan miso ramen!

Nutrition Information (per serving)

  • Calories: 320
  • Protein: 18g
  • Carbohydrates: 40g
  • Fat: 12g
  • Fiber: 6g
