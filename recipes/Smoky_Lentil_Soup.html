Smoky Lentil Soup

This Vegan Smoky Lentil Soup is a rich, hearty, and deeply flavorful dish that combines wholesome ingredients like tender lentils, juicy tomatoes, and a blend of smoky spices to create a perfect comfort food. The earthy lentils provide a satisfying base, while the smoky paprika and cumin give the soup a warm, smoky depth that enhances every bite. The combination of tomatoes, garlic, and onions adds sweetness and complexity, making this soup not only nourishing but also packed with layers of flavor. It's the perfect dish for cooler weather, but it can be enjoyed year-round as a filling, nutritious meal. Whether served with crusty bread or over a bed of greens, this soup offers both warmth and comfort in every spoonful. Plus, it's an excellent source of protein, fiber, and vitamins, making it a wholesome choice for anyone seeking a hearty, plant-based meal. For added richness, a drizzle of olive oil or a squeeze of fresh lemon can elevate the flavors. This Vegan Smoky Lentil Soup is sure to become a go-to recipe for cozy dinners or meal prepping for the week ahead.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried lentils (rinse well)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion (diced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 carrots (diced)
  • 2 celery stalks (diced)
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice (optional)
  • Fresh parsley (chopped, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the lentils: Begin by placing the dried lentils in a fine mesh sieve or colander. Rinse them thoroughly under cold running water to remove any debris or dust. Once rinsed, set the lentils aside to drain. This step is important to ensure that the lentils are clean and ready to absorb the flavors of the soup.
  2. Sauté the vegetables: In a large pot, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Once the oil is hot, add the diced onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté the vegetables for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the onions become translucent and the carrots and celery begin to soften. This step builds the base flavor of the soup and allows the vegetables to release their natural sweetness.
  3. Add garlic and spices: Add the minced garlic, smoked paprika, and ground cumin to the pot with the sautéed vegetables. Stir the mixture for 1-2 minutes, allowing the garlic to cook and the spices to become fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic, as it can become bitter. The spices will add depth and smokiness to the soup, enhancing the overall flavor.
  4. Add liquids and lentils: Pour in 4 cups of vegetable broth and 1 can of diced tomatoes (with their juices) into the pot. Stir to combine the ingredients. Next, add the rinsed lentils and a bay leaf. The liquid will begin to deglaze the pot, loosening any bits of vegetables or spices stuck to the bottom. Stir everything together to ensure the lentils are evenly distributed and submerged in the liquid.
  5. Simmer: Bring the soup to a boil over high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low to maintain a gentle simmer. Cover the pot and allow the soup to simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the lentils are tender. Stir the soup occasionally to prevent the lentils from sticking to the bottom of the pot. The lentils will absorb the broth, becoming soft and flavorful.
  6. Finish the soup: After the lentils have cooked and softened, remove the bay leaf from the pot. Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning by adding salt and pepper to your preference. If you'd like a touch of acidity to balance the flavors, add 1 tablespoon of lemon juice. Stir well to combine. Serve the soup hot, garnished with freshly chopped parsley for a burst of freshness and color.

Nutrition Information (per serving)

  • Calories: 180
  • Protein: 12g
  • Carbohydrates: 32g
  • Fat: 5g
  • Fiber: 12g
