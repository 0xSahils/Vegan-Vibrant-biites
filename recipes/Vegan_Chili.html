Vegan Chili

Warm up with a hearty bowl of Vegan Chili, a comforting dish that's as satisfying as it is wholesome. This plant-based chili is packed with protein-rich beans, like kidney, black, and pinto, simmered to perfection in a rich tomato base. The addition of vibrant vegetables such as bell peppers, onions, and diced tomatoes adds layers of flavor and nutrition, making this chili a nutritious and filling meal. The secret to its bold taste lies in the perfect blend of spices, including smoky paprika, earthy cumin, chili powder, and a hint of cayenne for a subtle kick. Each spoonful delivers a delightful combination of smoky, tangy, and slightly spicy flavors, complemented by the natural sweetness of the vegetables. For added depth, a splash of lime juice or a dollop of vegan sour cream brings a refreshing contrast to the robust flavors. Perfect for meal prep or a cozy family dinner, this Vegan Chili is versatile and easy to customize. Serve it with a side of warm cornbread, over rice, or enjoy it as is with your favorite toppings like avocado slices, chopped cilantro, or crunchy tortilla chips. Whether you're vegan or just looking for a delicious and healthy recipe, this chili will quickly become a go-to comfort food!

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 zucchini, chopped
  • 2 cans (15 oz) kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (6 oz) tomato paste
  • Roasted Tofu: How to make roasted Tofu
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish (optional)
  • Fresh lime wedges, for serving (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Vegetables: Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and bell pepper, and saut√© for about 5 minutes until softened. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 1 minute, until fragrant.
  2. Add the Zucchini and Spices: Stir in the chopped zucchini, chili powder, cumin, and smoked paprika. Cook for about 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the Beans and Tomatoes: Add the kidney beans, black beans, diced tomatoes, tomato paste, and vegetable broth to the pot. Stir to combine, ensuring the tomato paste is well incorporated.
  4. Simmer: Bring the chili to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let the chili simmer for about 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally. If you prefer a thicker chili, let it cook for a longer time.
  5. Season to Taste: Taste the chili and add salt and pepper as needed. For extra heat, you can add some cayenne pepper or hot sauce.
  6. Serve: Ladle the chili into bowls and garnish with fresh cilantro and tofu and a squeeze of lime, if desired. Serve with cornbread, rice, or tortilla chips for a complete meal.

Nutrition Information (per serving)

  • Calories: 320
  • Protein: 15g
  • Carbohydrates: 50g
  • Fat: 8g
  • Fiber: 12g
  • Sodium: 500mg
