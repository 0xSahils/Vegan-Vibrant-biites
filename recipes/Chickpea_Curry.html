Vegan Chickpea Curry

This Vegan Chickpea Curry is a heartwarming dish packed with bold flavors and nourishing ingredients. Tender chickpeas are simmered in a rich, creamy coconut milk base infused with aromatic spices like cumin, turmeric, and garam masala. The vibrant combination of flavors is perfectly balanced, offering a delightful blend of heat, creaminess, and subtle sweetness. Loaded with wholesome chickpeas, this curry is not only satisfying but also a fantastic source of plant-based protein and fiber. It's a versatile dish that pairs beautifully with steamed rice, naan, or even quinoa, making it a perfect meal for cozy dinners or meal prep. Whether you’re a seasoned curry lover or new to plant-based cuisine, this recipe will quickly become a household favorite. Plus, it’s easy to customize with your choice of vegetables or spice level, ensuring a dish that suits your taste perfectly.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked chickpeas: The star ingredient, packed with protein and fiber.
  • 1 tbsp coconut oil: Adds a rich, tropical flavor.
  • 1 onion, diced: Provides a savory base for the curry.
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced: For an aromatic punch.
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated: Adds warmth and depth to the curry.
  • 2 tbsp curry powder: A blend of spices to create the signature curry flavor.
  • 1 tsp ground cumin: Enhances the earthiness of the dish.
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder: Adds vibrant color and health benefits.
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk: For a creamy and luscious sauce.
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes: Adds acidity and balances the richness.
  • 1 cup vegetable broth:(How to make vegan broth here). To adjust the consistency of the curry.
  • 1 cup spinach or kale: Adds a burst of color and nutrients.
  • Salt and pepper: To taste.
  • Cooked rice or naan: For serving alongside the curry.

Instructions

  1. Sauté the aromatics: Heat coconut oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced onion and sauté until softened, about 5 minutes. Stir in the garlic and ginger, cooking for another 2 minutes.
  2. Add the spices: Sprinkle in the curry powder, cumin, and turmeric. Stir well and toast the spices for 1 minute to release their flavors.
  3. Combine liquids: Pour in the coconut milk, diced tomatoes, and vegetable broth. Stir to combine and bring the mixture to a gentle simmer.
  4. Add the chickpeas: Stir in the cooked chickpeas and let them simmer in the sauce for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  5. Add greens: Stir in the spinach or kale and cook for 2-3 minutes, just until wilted.
  6. Season and serve: Taste the curry and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. Serve hot with cooked rice or naan bread. Enjoy!

Nutrition Information (per serving)

  • Calories: 380
  • Protein: 12g
  • Carbohydrates: 34g
  • Fat: 20g
  • Fiber: 8g
