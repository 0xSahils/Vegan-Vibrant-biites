Vegan Sloppy Joes

Savor the bold and hearty flavors of Vegan Sloppy Joes, a plant-based take on the classic sandwich. This recipe features a savory, tangy filling made with lentils or textured vegetable protein (TVP), combined with a rich tomato-based sauce seasoned with smoky spices and a hint of sweetness. Piled high on a soft bun, it’s the ultimate comfort food that’s messy in all the best ways. Perfect for quick weeknight dinners, game-day gatherings, or family meals, Vegan Sloppy Joes are as satisfying as they are easy to prepare. The protein-packed filling is not only delicious but also nutritious, making it a great option for plant-based eaters and anyone looking to add more wholesome ingredients to their meals. This versatile recipe allows for customization—spice it up with some chili flakes, add diced bell peppers for extra crunch, or top with coleslaw for a refreshing contrast. Serve with a side of crispy fries, a fresh salad, or some pickles to round out the meal. Vegan Sloppy Joes are a nostalgic classic brought to life with a modern, plant-based twist. Enjoy all the flavors and textures of this beloved dish while keeping it entirely vegan!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried lentils: Cooked (or 2 cups canned lentils).
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil: For sautéing.
  • 1 onion: Diced.
  • 1 bell pepper: Chopped (any color).
  • 2 cloves garlic: Minced.
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes: For the base of the sauce.
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste: For depth of flavor.
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce: For savory richness.
  • 2 tablespoons maple syrup: Adds a touch of sweetness.
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika: For smokiness.
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder: For mild heat.
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin: For earthy flavor.
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper: To taste.
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar: Adds tanginess.
  • 4 burger buns: For serving, toasted if desired.
  • Optional: Pickles or fresh parsley for garnish.

Instructions

  1. Prepare the lentils: If using dried lentils, cook them according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Sauté the vegetables: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, bell pepper, and garlic, and sauté for 5-6 minutes, until softened.
  3. Add the sauce ingredients: Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, soy sauce, maple syrup, smoked paprika, chili powder, cumin, and black pepper. Mix well.
  4. Combine the lentils: Add the cooked lentils to the skillet and stir everything together. Let the mixture simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the sauce to thicken.
  5. Adjust the seasoning: Taste the mixture and adjust the seasonings if needed. Add apple cider vinegar for a tangy kick.
  6. Assemble the sandwiches: Spoon the lentil mixture onto the bottom half of each toasted burger bun. Top with pickles or fresh parsley if desired. Serve immediately.
