Vegan Empanadas

Enjoy a delicious and savory Vegan Empanada, filled with a flavorful blend of seasoned vegetables and plant-based proteins. These golden, flaky pockets are the perfect balance of crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, making them a delightful appetizer, snack, or even a main dish. The filling can be customized with a variety of ingredients such as spiced potatoes, lentils, black beans, or even vegan meat alternatives, combined with onions, garlic, and a mix of herbs and spices. Once wrapped in a tender, homemade or store-bought dough, they are baked to perfection or fried for extra crunch. Vegan Empanadas are incredibly versatile and can be paired with a variety of dipping sauces, such as a tangy tomato salsa or creamy avocado dip. They make for a great party snack or an easy meal for a cozy night in, and can also be prepped in advance and frozen for later use. Whether you’re serving them for a casual dinner or as part of a festive gathering, these empanadas are sure to be a hit with anyone, regardless of dietary preferences.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour: For the dough.
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt: For flavor.
  • 1/4 cup olive oil: For the dough.
  • 1/2 cup water: To bring the dough together.
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar: Adds flakiness to the dough.
  • 1 cup cooked black beans: Mashed or whole.
  • 1 cup corn kernels: Fresh or frozen.
  • 1 onion: Finely chopped.
  • 2 cloves garlic: Minced.
  • 1/2 bell pepper: Chopped.
  • 1 teaspoon cumin: For earthy flavor.
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika: For smokiness.
  • 1/4 teaspoon chili powder: For mild heat.
  • 1/2 cup chopped cilantro: Fresh for garnish and flavor.
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil: For sautéing the vegetables.
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce: Adds depth of flavor.
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice: Adds brightness to the filling.
  • 1/4 cup water: To help cook the filling.
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds: Optional, for garnish.

Instructions

  1. Make the dough: In a large bowl, combine flour and salt. Add olive oil and mix with your fingers until crumbly. Slowly add water and vinegar, kneading the dough until it comes together. Cover and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the filling: Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add onion, garlic, and bell pepper. Sauté for 5-7 minutes until softened. Add black beans, corn, cumin, paprika, chili powder, soy sauce, lime juice, and water. Stir and cook for another 5 minutes, until everything is well combined and heated through. Remove from heat and stir in cilantro.
  3. Assemble the empanadas: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Roll out the dough on a floured surface and cut into 4-inch circles. Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each circle. Fold the dough over to create a half-moon shape and press the edges with a fork to seal.
  4. Bake: Place the empanadas on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Brush the tops with olive oil and bake for 20-25 minutes or until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Serve: Allow to cool slightly before serving. Optionally, garnish with sesame seeds and extra cilantro.
