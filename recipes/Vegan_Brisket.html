Vegan Brisket

This Vegan Brisket is a mouthwatering plant-based alternative to traditional brisket, perfect for those seeking a hearty and satisfying dish. Made with tender, shredded jackfruit, this recipe offers a satisfying texture that mimics the classic meat version. The jackfruit is marinated and simmered in a rich, savory barbecue sauce that adds layers of smoky, tangy, and sweet flavors. The result is a delicious vegan brisket that is perfect for a family meal, weekend gatherings, or as the centerpiece for a holiday feast. Whether served on a bun for a vegan barbecue sandwich or as a main dish alongside roasted veggies, this vegan brisket is sure to impress. It's an ideal option for those following a plant-based diet or anyone looking to explore more plant-based meals without compromising on flavor or satisfaction. Enjoy this flavorful dish with your favorite sides or as part of a festive spread!

Ingredients

  • 2 cans young green jackfruit in brine: Rinsed and drained.
  • 1 medium onion: Thinly sliced.
  • 3 cloves garlic: Minced.
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce: For umami flavor.
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar: Adds tanginess.
  • 2 tbsp maple syrup: For sweetness.
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika: For a smoky flavor.
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin: Adds depth of flavor.
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper: For seasoning.
  • 1 cup barbecue sauce: Choose your favorite brand or homemade.
  • 1 tbsp olive oil: For sautéing.
  • Salt to taste: For seasoning.

Instructions

  1. Prepare the jackfruit: Drain and rinse the jackfruit, removing the seeds and tough core. Shred the jackfruit with your hands or a fork into a pulled meat texture.
  2. Sauté the onions and garlic: Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the onions and garlic, sautéing until fragrant and softened, about 3-4 minutes.
  3. Combine the jackfruit and seasonings: Add the shredded jackfruit to the skillet, along with soy sauce, apple cider vinegar, maple syrup, smoked paprika, cumin, black pepper, and salt. Stir to combine and cook for 5-7 minutes, allowing the jackfruit to absorb the flavors.
  4. Add the barbecue sauce: Pour in the barbecue sauce and stir until the jackfruit is fully coated. Cook for an additional 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the sauce to thicken.
  5. Serve: Serve the vegan brisket on sandwiches, tacos, or over rice. Enjoy with your favorite sides or as a standalone dish!
