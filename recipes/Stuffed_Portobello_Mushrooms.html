Stuffed Portobello Mushrooms

These stuffed portobello mushrooms are the perfect vegan dish for a light yet satisfying meal. Filled with quinoa, spinach, garlic, and herbs, they are a savory treat. Whether served as a main or a side dish, they are sure to be a crowd-pleaser!

Ingredients

  • 4 large portobello mushrooms: Gently cleaned and stems removed.
  • 1/2 cup quinoa: Rinsed and cooked.
  • 2 cups fresh spinach: Chopped.
  • 2 cloves garlic: Minced.
  • 1/4 cup sun-dried tomatoes: Chopped.
  • 1/4 cup vegan parmesan: Grated, optional for topping.
  • 1 tbsp olive oil: For sautéing.
  • 1 tsp dried oregano: Adds an aromatic flavor.
  • 1/2 tsp salt: To taste.
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper: To taste.
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar: Optional, for extra flavor.
  • Fresh parsley: Chopped, for garnish.

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the quinoa: Rinse and cook the quinoa according to package instructions. Set aside to cool slightly.
  3. Sauté the spinach: In a pan, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and cook for 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Add the spinach and sauté until wilted, about 3-4 minutes.
  4. Prepare the stuffing: In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, sautéed spinach, sun-dried tomatoes, oregano, salt, and pepper. If using balsamic vinegar, add it now. Mix until well combined.
  5. Stuff the mushrooms: Spoon the quinoa mixture into the portobello caps, pressing gently to pack the stuffing.
  6. Bake: Place the stuffed mushrooms on a baking sheet and bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes until the mushrooms are tender and the stuffing is heated through. Optionally, sprinkle with vegan parmesan during the last 5 minutes of baking.
  7. Serve: Garnish with fresh parsley and serve hot as a main or side dish!
