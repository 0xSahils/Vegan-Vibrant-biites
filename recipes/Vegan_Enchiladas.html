Vegan Enchiladas

Enjoy a delicious and savory Vegan Empanada, filled with a flavorful blend of seasoned vegetables and plant-based proteins. These golden, flaky pockets are the perfect balance of crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, making them a delightful appetizer, snack, or even a main dish. The filling can be customized with a variety of ingredients such as spiced potatoes, lentils, black beans, or even vegan meat alternatives, combined with onions, garlic, and a mix of herbs and spices. Once wrapped in a tender, homemade or store-bought dough, they are baked to perfection or fried for extra crunch. Vegan Empanadas are incredibly versatile and can be paired with a variety of dipping sauces, such as a tangy tomato salsa or creamy avocado dip. They make for a great party snack or an easy meal for a cozy night in, and can also be prepped in advance and frozen for later use. Whether you’re serving them for a casual dinner or as part of a festive gathering, these empanadas are sure to be a hit with anyone, regardless of dietary preferences.

Ingredients

  • 8-10 corn tortillas
  • 1 cup cooked black beans (or your preferred beans)
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes (canned or fresh)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups vegan enchilada sauce (red or green)
  • 1 cup vegan cheese shreds (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Lime wedges for serving
  • Vegan sour cream or guacamole (optional, for serving)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven: Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and lightly grease a baking dish (9x13-inch works well).
  2. Prepare the filling: In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion, bell pepper, and zucchini. Sauté for about 5-7 minutes until softened.
  3. Season the vegetables: Add the ground cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, onion powder, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Stir well to coat the vegetables in the spices.
  4. Add the beans and corn: Add the black beans, corn, and diced tomatoes to the skillet. Stir to combine and cook for an additional 3-4 minutes until everything is heated through. Remove from heat.
  5. Prepare the tortillas: Warm the corn tortillas in the microwave for 20-30 seconds or heat them in a dry skillet to make them more pliable. This will help prevent the tortillas from cracking when you roll them.
  6. Assemble the enchiladas: Spread about 1/2 cup of enchilada sauce on the bottom of the prepared baking dish. Take a tortilla and fill it with a generous amount of the vegetable-bean filling. Roll it up tightly and place it seam-side down in the dish. Repeat for all tortillas.
  7. Top with sauce and cheese: Pour the remaining enchilada sauce over the rolled tortillas and sprinkle with vegan cheese shreds if using.
  8. Bake the enchiladas: Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the sauce is bubbly and the cheese has melted (if using).
  9. Serve: Remove from the oven and garnish with fresh cilantro, lime wedges, and optional vegan sour cream or guacamole. Serve warm.

Nutrition Information (per serving)

  • Calories: 250
  • Protein: 10g
  • Carbohydrates: 40g
  • Fat: 8g
  • Fiber: 6g
