Vegan Spicy Sesame Noodles

These Vegan Spicy Sesame Noodles are a delightful combination of rich, savory flavors with a spicy kick. The sesame sauce is creamy and perfectly balanced with soy sauce, garlic, and ginger, creating a mouthwatering dressing for the noodles. This dish is quick to prepare, making it ideal for weeknight meals, meal prep, or a satisfying lunch. With a delightful mix of textures and flavors, including the crunch from roasted sesame seeds and the heat from chili paste, these noodles will quickly become a favorite in your kitchen. Serve them hot or cold, topped with fresh vegetables or tofu for added protein.

Ingredients

  • 200g rice noodles or your preferred noodles
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free version)
  • 2 tablespoons sesame paste (or tahini)
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup (or agave syrup for vegan sweetener)
  • 1 tablespoon sriracha or chili paste (adjust to taste for spice level)
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 2 teaspoons toasted sesame seeds
  • 1/2 cup green onions, chopped
  • Optional: 1/2 cup shredded carrots or cucumber for garnish
  • Optional: Tofu, tempeh, or edamame for protein (serve on top)

Instructions

  1. Cook the noodles: Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Add the rice noodles and cook according to package instructions, usually about 4-5 minutes. Drain and rinse under cold water to stop the cooking process. Set aside.
  2. Prepare the sesame sauce: In a bowl, combine the sesame paste (or tahini), soy sauce, rice vinegar, maple syrup, sriracha (or chili paste), lime juice, grated ginger, and minced garlic. Whisk together until smooth. If the sauce is too thick, add a little water to reach your desired consistency.
  3. Toss the noodles: In a large bowl, combine the cooked noodles with the sesame sauce. Toss well to coat the noodles evenly with the sauce.
  4. Garnish and serve: Top the noodles with toasted sesame seeds, chopped green onions, and any additional garnishes like shredded carrots or cucumber. If you're adding protein like tofu, tempeh, or edamame, place them on top or mix them in. Serve immediately or chill for a cold noodle salad.

Nutrition Information (per serving)

  • Calories: 320
  • Protein: 8g
  • Carbohydrates: 45g
  • Fat: 15g
  • Fiber: 4g
  • Sodium: 600mg
  • Vitamin A: 10% DV
  • Iron: 15% DV
