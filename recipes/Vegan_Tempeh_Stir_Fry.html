Vegan Tempeh Stir Fry

This Vegan Tempeh Fry is a flavorful and protein-packed dish that’s quick to prepare and perfect for any meal. With crispy edges and a savory glaze, this dish showcases tempeh in a deliciously satisfying way. Tempeh, a nutrient-dense soy product, is marinated in a blend of soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and a touch of maple syrup, infusing it with rich, umami flavors. Sliced into thin strips or cubes, the tempeh absorbs the marinade beautifully, ensuring each bite is bursting with taste. The cooking process is simple—pan-frying the marinated tempeh in a hot skillet until it develops a golden-brown crust. This quick frying technique enhances the texture, creating a delightful contrast between the crispy exterior and the tender interior. Serve this Vegan Tempeh Fry over steamed rice, quinoa, or alongside stir-fried vegetables for a wholesome and balanced meal. It’s also a fantastic addition to salads, wraps, or grain bowls, adding a hearty and nutritious element. Whether you’re looking for a quick weeknight dinner or a versatile protein option to complement your meals, this Vegan Tempeh Fry is sure to impress with it

Ingredients

  • 1 block of tempeh (about 8 ounces), cubed: A protein-packed, fermented soy product with a nutty flavor.
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch: Helps to crisp up the tempeh when pan-fried.
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free): Adds a salty, umami flavor to the dish.
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil: A nutty oil perfect for stir-fry dishes.
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables: Use a colorful blend of bell peppers, broccoli, snap peas, and carrots.
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced: Adds a rich, aromatic base to the stir-fry.
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger: Provides a warm, spicy kick.
  • 3 tbsp stir-fry sauce: A mix of soy sauce, hoisin sauce, and a touch of chili sauce.
  • 1 tsp sesame seeds: For garnish and added crunch.
  • Cooked rice or noodles: Serve alongside the stir-fry for a complete meal.

Instructions

  1. Prep the tempeh: Toss the cubed tempeh with cornstarch and 1 tablespoon of soy sauce. Set aside to marinate for 10 minutes.
  2. Cook the tempeh: Heat 1 tablespoon of sesame oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the tempeh and cook until golden and crispy on all sides. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. Sauté vegetables: In the same skillet, add the garlic and ginger. Sauté for 1 minute, then add the mixed vegetables. Stir-fry until tender-crisp.
  4. Add the sauce: Return the tempeh to the skillet and pour in the stir-fry sauce. Toss to coat everything evenly and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  5. Serve: Sprinkle with sesame seeds and serve hot over rice or noodles. Enjoy!

Nutrition Information (per serving)

  • Calories: 320
  • Protein: 18g
  • Carbohydrates: 28g
  • Fat: 12g
  • Fiber: 4g
