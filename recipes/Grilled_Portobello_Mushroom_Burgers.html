Grilled Portobello Mushroom Burger

Discover the ultimate vegan burger experience with this Grilled Portobello Mushroom Burger. Featuring large, meaty Portobello mushrooms marinated in a flavorful blend of olive oil, garlic, balsamic vinegar, and a touch of soy sauce, this dish captures a smoky, umami-rich essence that rivals traditional patties. Perfectly grilled to bring out their natural flavors and paired with a toasted bun, these mushrooms are tender on the inside and slightly crisp on the edges.

Add fresh toppings like crisp lettuce, juicy tomato slices, red onions, and creamy avocado for a burst of freshness. To elevate the taste even further, spread vegan aioli or tangy mustard on the bun. Whether served with a side of sweet potato fries or a refreshing salad, this burger makes a satisfying meal for any occasion.

Ideal for barbecues, weeknight dinners, or casual gatherings, this Grilled Portobello Mushroom Burger is not just a recipe but an experience. It’s packed with nutrients, low in calories, and offers a healthy, plant-based alternative without compromising on taste. Even meat lovers will be impressed by the rich, smoky flavors and hearty texture of this vegan favorite. Try it today for a meal that’s wholesome, delicious, and unforgettable!

Ingredients

  • 4 large portobello mushrooms: Cleaned and stems removed.
  • 2 tbsp olive oil: For brushing the mushrooms.
  • 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar: For added flavor.
  • 1 tsp garlic powder: Seasoning for the mushrooms.
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika: For a smoky touch.
  • Salt and pepper: To taste.
  • 4 burger buns: Toasted.
  • Lettuce: Fresh and crisp.
  • Sliced tomato: Ripe and juicy.
  • Vegan mayonnaise or sauce: Optional, for spreading.

Instructions

  1. Prepare the marinade: In a small mixing bowl, combine 3 tablespoons of olive oil, 2 tablespoons of balsamic vinegar, 1 teaspoon of garlic powder, 1/2 teaspoon of smoked paprika, 1/4 teaspoon of salt, and a pinch of black pepper. Whisk until well blended to create a flavorful marinade.
  2. Marinate the mushrooms: Clean the Portobello mushrooms by gently wiping them with a damp paper towel. Remove the stems for a flat grilling surface. Using a brush or spoon, generously coat both sides of the mushrooms with the marinade. Place them on a plate and let them sit for 15-20 minutes to absorb the flavors.
  3. Grill the mushrooms: Preheat a grill or grill pan over medium heat. Once hot, place the marinated mushrooms cap-side down on the grill. Cook for 5-7 minutes per side, or until the mushrooms are tender, slightly charred, and release a smoky aroma. For deeper flavor, baste the mushrooms with any remaining marinade while grilling.
  4. Toast the buns: Slice your burger buns in half and lightly toast them on the grill for 1-2 minutes until golden and crisp. This step adds texture and enhances the overall flavor.
  5. Assemble the burgers: Start by spreading a generous layer of vegan mayonnaise, aioli, or your favorite sauce on the bottom half of each bun. Add a layer of fresh lettuce, followed by the grilled Portobello mushroom cap. Top with juicy tomato slices, red onion rings, and avocado if desired. Finish with the top bun.
  6. Serve and enjoy: Serve your Grilled Portobello Mushroom Burger warm alongside a side of crispy sweet potato fries, a refreshing salad, or any side of your choice. Dig in and savor the rich, smoky, and wholesome flavors!

Nutrition Information (per serving)

  • Calories: 290
  • Protein: 8g
  • Carbohydrates: 35g
  • Fat: 12g
  • Fiber: 4g
