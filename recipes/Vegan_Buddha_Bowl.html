Vegan Buddha Bowl – Let’s Make a Colorful Meal!

-->

A vibrant and nourishing meal in a bowl, the Vegan Buddha Bowl is a versatile and customizable dish packed with colorful, wholesome ingredients. Perfect for a healthy lunch or dinner, it combines a variety of textures and flavors that satisfy both the body and the taste buds. The base typically features a grain like quinoa, brown rice, or couscous, layered with fresh vegetables, plant-based proteins, and topped with a c reamy dressing. Common ingredients include roasted sweet potatoes, sautéed greens (such as spinach or kale), crunchy chickpeas, avocado, and shredded carrots. Drizzle with tahini sauce, a tangy lemon dressing, or your favorite vegan dressing to tie everything together. This bowl is not only nutrient-dense but also highly customizable, allowing you to adjust the ingredients to your preferences or what’s in season. It’s a perfect meal for meal prepping or a quick, nourishing dinner after a long day.

What We Need:

  • 1 cup quinoa, cooked
  • 1 cup chickpeas, roasted
  • 1/2 cup cucumber, sliced
  • 1/2 cup carrots, shredded
  • 1/2 cup avocado, sliced
  • 1/4 cup red cabbage, shredded
  • 2 tbsp tahini
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

How to Make Your Buddha Bowl:

  1. Cook the Quinoa: First, cook the quinoa according to the instructions on the package. Usually, you’ll need 2 cups of water for every 1 cup of quinoa. Bring it to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for 15 minutes. Let it cool down a little after it’s cooked.
  2. Roast the Chickpeas: Drain the chickpeas and pat them dry. Toss them with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast them in the oven at 400°F (200°C) for 25-30 minutes until they’re crispy and golden.
  3. Prepare the Veggies: While the quinoa and chickpeas are cooking, slice the cucumber, shred the carrots, and cut the avocado into slices. Also, shred the red cabbage and set it aside.
  4. Make the Tahini Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the tahini, lemon juice, olive oil, salt, and pepper. If it’s too thick, add a little water to make it creamy.
  5. Assemble the Buddha Bowl: In a large bowl, place the cooked quinoa at the bottom. Arrange the roasted chickpeas, cucumber, shredded carrots, avocado slices, and red cabbage on top. Drizzle the tahini dressing over everything and garnish with fresh parsley.
  6. Enjoy! Now your Vegan Buddha Bowl is ready to enjoy. It’s colorful, healthy, and full of yummy flavors!

Nutrition Information:

  • Calories: 400
  • Protein: 14 grams
  • Carbohydrates: 45 grams
  • Fat: 18 grams
  • Fiber: 10 grams
  • Iron: 3 mg
  • Vitamin C: 25 mg
