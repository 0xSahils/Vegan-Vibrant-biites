Vegan Pineapple Fried Rice

Enjoy this vibrant Vegan Pineapple Fried Rice, a delicious and tropical twist on the classic fried rice. Made with fragrant jasmine rice, fresh pineapple chunks, colorful vegetables, and a blend of savory seasonings, this dish brings together sweet, salty, and umami flavors in every bite. The pineapple adds a refreshing sweetness, while the vegetables like peas, carrots, and bell peppers bring in texture and color. This fried rice is cooked in a wok with garlic and soy sauce for extra depth of flavor, and can be topped with fresh cilantro, green onions, and a squeeze of lime for a bright finish. It's a quick, easy, and satisfying meal that's perfect for lunch or dinner, and a great way to use up leftover rice. This dish is naturally vegan, gluten-free, and full of nutrients, making it a great choice for a healthy, plant-based meal. Plus, it's easily customizable by adding tofu, tempeh, or your favorite plant-based protein to boost its protein content. Whether you’re looking for a quick weeknight meal or a vibrant dish for a special occasion, this Vegan Pineapple Fried Rice is sure to impress.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked jasmine rice (preferably day-old rice)
  • 1 cup pineapple chunks (fresh or canned, drained)
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 1/2 cup diced bell pepper
  • 1/2 cup peas (frozen or fresh)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1/4 cup green onions (chopped, for garnish)
  • 1 tbsp lime juice (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the rice: If using fresh rice, spread it on a tray and let it cool. Day-old rice works best for fried rice.
  2. Cook the vegetables: Heat sesame oil in a large pan or wok over medium heat. Add onions and bell pepper, and sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook for another minute.
  3. Fry the rice: Add the cooked rice to the pan and stir-fry for about 5 minutes, breaking up any clumps.
  4. Season the rice: Stir in soy sauce, turmeric, and black pepper. Add the peas and pineapple chunks, and cook for another 2-3 minutes until heated through.
  5. Finish and serve: Remove from heat, stir in lime juice (optional), and garnish with green onions. Serve warm.

Nutrition Information (per serving)

  • Calories: 250
  • Protein: 4g
  • Carbohydrates: 48g
  • Fat: 7g
  • Fiber: 3g
