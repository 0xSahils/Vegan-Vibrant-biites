Vegan Lentil Soup

A hearty, comforting bowl of Vegan Lentil Soup is perfect for warming up on a cold day. This nutrient-packed soup is made with tender lentils, vegetables, and aromatic spices, creating a filling and flavorful dish. Lentils are a great source of plant-based protein and fiber, making this soup not only delicious but also satisfying and nourishing. The base is typically made with onions, garlic, carrots, celery, and tomatoes, simmered with vegetable broth and seasoned with cumin, turmeric, coriander, and bay leaves for a rich depth of flavor. You can also add greens like spinach or kale for an extra boost of nutrients. This soup is naturally vegan, gluten-free, and easy to make, offering a warm and wholesome meal that's perfect for lunch, dinner, or meal prepping. It’s a simple yet flavorful dish that’s sure to become a staple in your plant-based cooking repertoire.
This recipe is not only filling and packed with protein but also offers a comforting and flavorful experience with every spoonful!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried lentils, rinsed: The heart of this Bolognese, providing a rich, meaty texture and a dose of plant-based protein.
  • 1 medium onion, diced: Adds a sweet, aromatic base to the sauce.
  • 2 carrots, peeled and chopped: For natural sweetness and a touch of vibrant color.
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped: Complements the carrots and onion for a classic mirepoix flavor.
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced: Brings a robust, savory depth to the dish.
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes: The acidic backbone of the sauce, balancing out the other flavors.
  • 4 cups vegetable broth: Provides the liquid for simmering the lentils to perfection.
  • 1 tsp cumin: Adds warm, earthy notes that enhance the sauce's complexity.
  • 1 tsp turmeric: For subtle spice and golden hues.
  • 1/2 tsp paprika: Introduces a smoky undertone to the Bolognese.
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper: Adds a hint of heat and depth to the seasoning.
  • 1/2 tsp salt, or to taste: Enhances the overall flavor profile.
  • 1 tbsp olive oil: Used for sautéing the vegetables, adding richness and smoothness.
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish (optional): A fresh, herbaceous touch to brighten the dish before serving.

Instructions

  1. Prepare the vegetables: Finely dice the onion, carrots, celery, and garlic to ensure an even texture in the sauce.
  2. Sauté the base: Heat olive oil in a large skillet or pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion, carrots, and celery, and sauté for 5-7 minutes until they become tender and aromatic.
  3. Add the garlic: Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute, ensuring it doesn’t burn.
  4. Add lentils and seasoning: Rinse the lentils under cold water and add them to the pot. Sprinkle in Italian seasoning, smoked paprika, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Stir well to coat the lentils with the spices.
  5. Incorporate the liquids: Pour in the canned tomatoes (with their juices) and vegetable broth. Stir to combine and bring the mixture to a gentle boil.
  6. Simmer the sauce: Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let the sauce simmer for 25-30 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking, and add more broth if needed for a thinner consistency.
  7. Blend and adjust: For a smoother sauce, use an immersion blender to partially blend the mixture, leaving some lentils whole for texture. Adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or a touch of sugar if the tomatoes are too acidic.
  8. Serve: Toss the Lentil Bolognese with your favorite pasta, garnish with fresh basil, and sprinkle with vegan Parmesan. Enjoy warm with crusty bread on the side.

Nutrition Information (per serving)

  • Calories: 180
  • Protein: 12g
  • Carbohydrates: 30g
  • Fat: 4g
  • Fiber: 10g

  • Vitamin A: 35% of Daily Value
