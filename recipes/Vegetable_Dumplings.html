Vegan Vegetable Dumplings

These Vegan Vegetable Dumplings are a delightful combination of tender wrappers filled with a flavorful medley of vegetables and seasonings. Perfect as an appetizer, snack, or main dish, these dumplings are versatile, healthy, and absolutely delicious. The filling is a colorful mix of finely chopped cabbage, carrots, mushrooms, and green onions, seasoned with soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger. The result is a perfectly balanced bite of savory, umami flavors with a hint of freshness from the vegetables. These dumplings can be steamed, pan-fried for a crispy texture, or boiled for a lighter option, making them adaptable to your preference. Whether served on their own, paired with a tangy dipping sauce, or included in a soup, they’re sure to be a crowd-pleaser. Making these dumplings is a fun and rewarding process, perfect for sharing with family or friends as a cooking project. They’re also freezer-friendly, making it easy to prepare a batch in advance for quick meals on busy days. With their delicate wrappers, savory filling, and customizable preparation methods, these Vegan Vegetable Dumplings are a must-try for anyone looking to enjoy a wholesome and satisfying plant-based dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup shredded carrots
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped green onions
  • 1 tbsp minced ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tsp rice vinegar
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 package dumpling wrappers (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch (for sealing the wrappers)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the filling: In a large bowl, combine the shredded cabbage, mushrooms, carrots, green onions, garlic, and ginger. Add soy sauce, sesame oil, rice vinegar, and black pepper. Toss everything together until evenly mixed.
  2. Assemble the dumplings: Place a small spoonful of the filling in the center of each dumpling wrapper. Lightly moisten the edges of the wrapper with water, then fold and seal the edges, creating a half-moon shape or pleated pouch.
  3. Steam the dumplings: Bring water to a boil in a steamer basket. Arrange the dumplings in the basket, ensuring they don’t touch each other. Steam for 8-10 minutes, or until the wrappers are translucent and the filling is cooked.
  4. Pan-fry the dumplings (optional): Heat a little oil in a pan over medium heat. Place the steamed dumplings in the pan and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Serve: Serve the dumplings with soy sauce or a dipping sauce of your choice. Garnish with sesame seeds or fresh cilantro if desired.

Nutrition Information (per serving)

  • Calories: 150
  • Protein: 4g
  • Carbohydrates: 30g
  • Fat: 3g
  • Fiber: 4g
