Vegan Tonkotsu Ramen

This Vegan Tonkotsu Ramen features a rich, creamy broth traditionally made with pork bones but here, we've created a plant-based version that is just as satisfying and comforting. The base of this ramen uses a blend of miso, soy milk, and a variety of umami-rich ingredients to recreate that signature tonkotsu depth and texture. The noodles are tender, soaking up the flavorful broth perfectly. Topped with sautéed mushrooms, green onions, and nori, this ramen is a true bowl of comfort that will have you craving more. Whether you're looking for a warming meal on a cold day or craving some authentic Japanese ramen, this vegan version is sure to impress!

Ingredients

  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cups unsweetened soy milk (or other plant-based milk)
  • 1/4 cup white miso paste
  • 2 tablespoons tahini
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce or tamari (for gluten-free option)
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 2 teaspoons sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of fresh ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons nutritional yeast
  • 1 tablespoon chili paste (optional, for heat)
  • 200g ramen noodles (gluten-free if desired)
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms (shiitake or button mushrooms)
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 sheet nori, cut into strips
  • 1/4 cup bamboo shoots (optional)
  • 1 soft-boiled or marinated tofu (for added protein, optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the broth: In a large pot, heat the sesame oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and ginger, sautéing until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add the vegetable broth, soy milk, white miso paste, tahini, soy sauce, rice vinegar, nutritional yeast, and chili paste (if using). Stir to combine and bring to a simmer. Let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, to allow the flavors to meld.
  2. Cook the noodles: While the broth is simmering, cook the ramen noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside, keeping them warm.
  3. Sauté the mushrooms: In a separate pan, heat a little sesame oil over medium heat. Add the sliced mushrooms and sauté for about 5-7 minutes until they are tender and slightly browned.
  4. Assemble the ramen: Divide the cooked ramen noodles between two bowls. Pour the hot tonkotsu broth over the noodles, ensuring they are fully covered. Top with sautéed mushrooms, green onions, nori strips, bamboo shoots (optional), and a soft-boiled or marinated tofu for extra protein.
  5. Serve: Serve the ramen hot, garnished with any additional toppings you desire. Enjoy this vegan version of tonkotsu ramen with a side of pickled vegetables or kimchi for an extra punch of flavor.
