Vegetable Tempura

Vegetable Tempura is a light and crispy Japanese-inspired dish that features fresh vegetables dipped in a delicate tempura batter and fried to golden perfection. This vegan-friendly recipe is a delightful way to enjoy a variety of vegetables with an irresistible crunch. The batter is made with simple ingredients like all-purpose flour, cornstarch, and cold sparkling water, which ensures a light and airy texture. Seasonal vegetables such as sweet potatoes, zucchini, bell peppers, broccoli, and mushrooms are sliced into bite-sized pieces, making them easy to coat and fry. To achieve the signature crispiness of tempura, the batter is kept cold, and the vegetables are fried in hot oil for just a few minutes until they turn golden and crispy. The result is a perfect balance of textures—crispy on the outside, tender on the inside. Served with a savory dipping sauce, such as soy sauce mixed with a touch of mirin and grated ginger, this dish is perfect as an appetizer, snack, or side dish. Vegetable Tempura is a versatile and satisfying way to enjoy vegetables, making it a hit at gatherings or as a special treat at home.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour: For the tempura batter.
  • 1/2 cup corn starch: To make the batter light and crispy.
  • 1 tsp baking powder: To make the batter airy.
  • 1/2 tsp salt: For seasoning the batter.
  • 1 cup cold sparkling water: For the batter's light texture.
  • 1 zucchini: Sliced into thin rounds.
  • 1 sweet potato: Cut into thin strips or rounds.
  • 1 carrot: Cut into thin strips.
  • 1/2 cup green beans: Trimmed and halved.
  • Vegetable oil: For frying (canola or sunflower oil works well).
  • Soy sauce: For dipping (optional).
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds: For garnish (optional).

Instructions

  1. Prepare the vegetables: Slice the zucchini, sweet potato, and carrot into thin rounds or strips. Trim the green beans to about 2-inch lengths.
  2. Make the batter: In a bowl, combine the flour, corn starch, baking powder, and salt. Slowly whisk in the cold sparkling water until the batter is smooth but not too thick. The batter should be slightly lumpy for the best texture.
  3. Heat the oil: Heat the vegetable oil in a deep pan or wok to about 350°F (175°C). The oil should be enough to submerge the vegetables.
  4. Coat and fry the vegetables: Dip the vegetables into the batter, ensuring they are fully coated. Fry them in batches, turning occasionally, until golden brown and crispy (about 3-4 minutes per batch). Be careful not to overcrowd the pan.
  5. Drain the excess oil: Remove the vegetables from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain any excess oil.
  6. Serve: Serve the tempura hot, garnished with sesame seeds and with soy sauce on the side for dipping.
