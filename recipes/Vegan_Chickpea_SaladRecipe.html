Vegan Chickpea Salad

This Vegan Chickpea Salad is a perfect balance of flavor, nutrition, and convenience. Rich in plant-based protein and loaded with fresh veggies like cucumber, bell pepper, and tomatoes, it’s an ideal choice for a quick lunch or light dinner. Each bite is a refreshing burst of flavor, with a hint of tang from lemon juice and a satisfying crunch from chopped vegetables. This salad is low in calories, high in fiber, and provides essential nutrients like iron and vitamin C, making it a healthy and delicious addition to any diet. Whether you’re a seasoned vegan or simply looking to add more plant-based meals to your routine, this chickpea salad is sure to be a hit.

Ingredients

  • 1 can (400g) chickpeas, rinsed and drained
  • 1 cup cucumber, diced
  • 1 cup bell pepper (any color), diced
  • 1/2 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tbsp freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp salt, or to taste
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper, or to taste

Let's Make It!

  1. Prepare the Vegetables: First, we take the cucumber, bell pepper, and red onion. We need to cut them into small pieces so we can eat them easily. Cut the cucumber into small slices, chop the bell pepper into little squares, and the onion into tiny pieces. Don’t forget to cut the tomatoes into halves!
  2. Put Everything in a Big Bowl: Now, grab a big bowl and put all the chopped veggies in it. Add the chickpeas too (they’re already cooked from the can!). Don’t forget the parsley—just rip it into small pieces and sprinkle it on top.
  3. Make the Dressing: In a tiny bowl, mix the olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper. This will make the salad taste really yummy. Stir it together with a spoon.
  4. Mix It All Up: Pour the dressing you made over the veggies and chickpeas. Now, gently mix everything with a spoon so the dressing covers all the pieces. Don’t squish the tomatoes, though!
  5. Taste and Adjust: Taste your salad and see if it needs a little more salt or lemon juice. You can add more if you want it to taste extra yummy!
  6. Serve and Enjoy: Now your salad is ready to eat! You can eat it right away or let it rest in the fridge for a little bit to make it even tastier.

Nutrition Information (What’s Good for You in This Salad)

  • Calories: 250 (not too much, just enough to feel full)
  • Protein: 8 grams (it helps your muscles grow!)
  • Carbohydrates: 30 grams (gives you energy to play)
  • Fat: 12 grams (good fats for your body)
  • Fiber: 7 grams (helps your tummy feel good)
  • Iron: Makes you strong!
  • Vitamin C: Helps you stay healthy!
