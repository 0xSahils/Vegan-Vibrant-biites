Vegan Pho

This Vegan Pho is a hearty and aromatic noodle soup that's perfect for any occasion. Made with a rich and flavorful vegetable broth, this pho is infused with warm spices like star anise, cinnamon, cloves, and ginger, creating a wonderfully complex taste. The broth is simmered to perfection, allowing the flavors to meld together beautifully, resulting in a deeply satisfying soup. Paired with soft rice noodles, this vegan pho is comforting and filling without being heavy. It's topped with fresh herbs like basil, cilantro, and mint, along with bean sprouts, lime wedges, and sliced chili, which add a burst of freshness and heat. The dish can be customized to your liking by adding a variety of vegetables, tofu, or mushrooms for extra protein and texture. Perfect for a cozy meal on a chilly day, this vegan pho offers all the comforting qualities of the traditional dish, but with a plant-based twist. It's nourishing, light, and full of flavor, making it a great choice for a healthy lunch or dinner. Whether you're a long-time pho lover or new to this Vietnamese classic, this vegan pho is sure to become a favorite in your recipe rotation.

Ingredients

  • 8 cups vegetable broth: (How to make vegan broth here).The base of the Pho, providing a rich and savory flavor.
  • 1 onion, halved: Adds sweetness and depth to the broth.
  • 4-5 slices ginger: For a warm, spicy undertone in the broth.
  • 2 cinnamon sticks: To give the Pho its signature aromatic flavor.
  • 3-4 star anise: Adds a sweet, licorice-like aroma to the broth.
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce: Adds saltiness and umami flavor.
  • 1 tbsp miso paste: Adds a depth of umami and saltiness.
  • 200g rice noodles: The traditional noodle for Pho, soft and chewy.
  • 1 cup mushrooms (shiitake or button), sliced: For a meaty texture and earthy flavor.
  • 1 cup tofu, cubed: Adds protein and a soft texture.
  • Fresh herbs: Basil, cilantro, and mint, for garnish.
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges: Adds a zesty kick when squeezed over the Pho.
  • 2-3 spring onions, sliced: For fresh, sharp flavor.
  • 1-2 chili peppers, sliced: Adds heat to the Pho (optional).
  • 1/4 cup bean sprouts: For crunch and freshness.
  • Sriracha and hoisin sauce: Optional, for extra flavor.

Instructions

  1. Prepare the broth: In a large pot, add vegetable broth, onion, ginger, cinnamon sticks, and star anise. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat and simmer for 30 minutes to allow the flavors to infuse.
  2. Strain the broth: After simmering, strain the broth to remove the onion, ginger, and spices. Return the broth to the pot.
  3. Prepare the noodles: Cook the rice noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  4. Cook the mushrooms and tofu: In a separate pan, sauté the mushrooms and tofu until golden brown. Set aside.
  5. Assemble the Pho: Divide the cooked noodles into bowls. Pour the hot broth over the noodles, then top with sautéed mushrooms, tofu, and fresh herbs.
  6. Garnish: Add sliced lime, spring onions, chili peppers, and bean sprouts on top of the Pho. Serve with Sriracha and hoisin sauce for extra flavor.
